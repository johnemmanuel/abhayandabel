Our Platform

Conference Logistics

  • Joint Sessions

    By giving Senate and Assembly the chance to debate together, the Saturday committee sessions could be much more engaging. It would also allow delegates to talk to people outside of their own committee as well.

  • Bill Recognition

    As of right now successfull bills must attain a high bucketing score, pass in their committee of origin, pass in the opposite commitee, and be approved by the Cabinet before they are passed. Such a monumental deserves more recognition on the YAG website and the YAG instagram account.

  • Bill Merging

    Many returning delegates know how a low bucketing score for your bill can prevent your bill from being heard at all. This can ruin your entire conference, but there is a solution. By allowing bill merging during conference, delegates with low bucketing scores could team up with each other so they can at least present a form of their bill at conference.

Updating Technology

  • Upgrading Wifi

    Every year, at least one of the gubernatorial candidates will bring up the need for better Wifi at conference. But the very paper oriented nature of conference has prevented this from happening. By using our experience this year, in an entirely virtual format, we can modernize YAG completely so that better wifi becomes a critical part of conference.

  • Conference Website / App

    The second part of modernizing YAG is setting up a website or an app to guide delegates during conference. Such a site would include a updated docketing system, a map and personal schedule of the conference , and a place to vote in elections. A better docketing system could tell you what stage in the approval process your bill is in, and upcoming bills could also be viewed ahead of time. The personalized map and schedule would tell you everything you need to know to be ready for each committee session. And with voting also taking place virtually, we could eliminate bill binders, conference folders, and paper ballots.

Increasing Outreach

  • Linking with MUN

    Despite being sister programs, MUN and YAG often do little to help each other grow. By making it easier for existing MUN delegations to join YAG, conference could easily gain hundreds of delegates. More delegates allows for more committees and a greater diversity of delegates from all over the state.

  • Individual Conference Attendees

    The greatest challenge to increasing the size of conference is that every increase has to be done by creating a delegation at a new school. Simply allowing delegates to attend independently is done through various YMCAs but such an option is not advertised anywhere. It is also limited to a select few delegates and is not open to many. By expanding this independent entry into the program, students from schools without YAG delegations can still enjoy conference. And given time, some of these students could start their own delegations and the program would continue to grow.
